# Marathon Race Day Nutrition Plan

## Pre-Race Meal (2-3 hours before)

**Goal: 200-300g carbs, low fiber, moderate protein**

- **Pasta with marinara sauce** (1.5-2 cups cooked pasta)
- Grilled chicken breast (100g)
- White bread with honey
- Banana
- Sports drink (500ml)

## During Race (every 45-60 min after hour 1)

- **Energy gels** (20-30g carbs each) OR
- **Sports drink** (6-8% carbs) - 500-750ml/hour
- **Energy chews** or dried fruit
- Water between carb intake (plain or electrolyte)

**Target: 30-60g carbs/hour**

## Hydration Strategy

- **Before race**: 400-600ml water (2-3 hours prior)
- **During race**: 150-250ml every 15-20 min (warm weather = more)
- **After race**: 1.5L for every kg of weight lost

## 3-Day Pre-Race Carb Loading

- Increase carbs to **10-12g per kg body weight** (720-864g daily)
- Reduce fiber/fat to avoid GI issues
- Reduce running intensity
- Stay well-hydrated

## Post-Race Recovery (within 30 min)

- Carbs + protein: chocolate milk, bagel with turkey, or sports drink + sandwich
- Continue hydrating with electrolytes

**Consult your sports nutritionist for personalized timing based on your training!**

# Nutrition Plan for Weightlifting

## Daily Calorie & Macro Targets

**Estimated Daily Calories:** 2,800–3,200 kcal
- **Protein:** 170–190g (2.0–2.2g per kg body weight)
- **Carbs:** 350–420g (4.1–5.0g per kg)
- **Fat:** 90–110g (1.0–1.3g per kg)

## Priority Foods

### Protein Sources (aim for 30-40g per meal)
- Chicken breast, lean beef, salmon
- Eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
- Protein powder (whey/casein)
- Legumes (lentils, chickpeas)

### Carbohydrates (fuel for training)
- Oats, rice, pasta, potatoes
- Sweet potatoes, quinoa
- Whole grain bread
- Fruits (bananas, berries)

### Healthy Fats
- Olive oil, avocados, nuts
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
- Nut butters

## Meal Timing Tips
- **Pre-workout:** Carbs + protein 2–3 hours before
- **Post-workout:** Protein + carbs within 1–2 hours (high priority)
- **Spread protein** across 4–5 meals for muscle synthesis

## Additional Notes
- **Hydration:** 3–4 liters water daily
- **Adjust calories** based on weekly progress (aim for 0.5–1 lb/week gain)
- **Consistency** matters more than perfection

# Vegan Nutrition Plan for Basketball Performance

## Daily Calorie & Macro Targets
- **Calories**: ~2,800-3,200/day (depending on training intensity)
- **Protein**: 110-130g/day (1.3-1.6g per kg bodyweight)
- **Carbs**: 350-420g/day (4-5g per kg)
- **Fats**: 75-90g/day (0.9-1.1g per kg)

## Protein Sources (Priority)
- Tofu & tempeh (15-19g protein per serving)
- Legumes: lentils, chickpeas, black beans (15-20g per cooked cup)
- Quinoa (8g per cooked cup)
- Nuts & seeds: hemp, pumpkin, sunflower (5-10g per 2 tbsp)
- Nutritional yeast (8g per 2 tbsp)
- Vegan protein powder (20-30g per scoop)

## Sample Daily Framework

**Breakfast**
- Oatmeal with hemp seeds, banana, almond butter

**Snack**
- Vegan protein shake with berries

**Lunch**
- Tofu stir-fry with quinoa and vegetables

**Pre-Training**
- Banana + dates or rice cakes

**Post-Training** (within 30 mins)
- Protein smoothie with carbs

**Dinner**
- Lentil pasta with tempeh bolognese

**Evening Snack**
- Nuts or hummus with veggies

## Key Supplements to Consider
- **Vitamin B12** (essential—not naturally available in vegan foods)
- **Creatine monohydrate** (supports muscle performance)
- **Vitamin D** (if limited sun exposure)
- Algae-based omega-3s (EPA/DHA)

**Recommendation**: Consult a sports dietitian to fine-tune this plan based on your specific training schedule.
